# GEO Gas Campaign: Gas Superheroes

## Campaign Position

GEO Gas are the everyday heroes who turn heating, plumbing, drainage and gas emergencies into calm, fixed, safe homes.

The campaign should feel like a real customer problem has been lifted into a comic-book world: familiar job situations, fast response, red-and-black superhero energy, and a clear reason to call or sign up.

## Lead Creative Direction

Use `public/cartoons/van1.jpeg` as the lead image and colour reference.

Visual system:
- Primary red: GEO Gas van red
- Deep black/charcoal: engineer uniforms and comic shadows
- White: van body, text fields, safety clarity
- Safety yellow: bursts, warnings, raffle/offer highlights
- Accent orange: emergency glow and comic action backgrounds

Tone:
- Fun, bold and memorable
- Confident but not silly
- Service-led, with safety and peace of mind always visible
- Comic-book drama for attention, practical proof in the copy

Core line:
**Your home emergency heroes, on call 24/7.**

Supporting lines:
- **From breakdown panic to boiler fixed.**
- **Heating, plumbing, drainage and gas cover with everyday heroes on call.**
- **Plans from GBP19 per month. Peace of mind, without the drama.**
- **Gas Safe registered. 24/7 helpline. 24hr response target.**

## Audience

Primary audience:
- Homeowners and landlords who want reliable boiler, heating, plumbing and gas support.
- Families who value fast, reassuring response.
- People who have had an unexpected repair bill and want predictable monthly cover.

Secondary audience:
- Landlords needing Gas Safety inspections, certificates and dependable emergency support.
- Local customers who respond well to recognisable vans, engineer faces and real job stories.

## Key Messages From Meeting Notes

Campaign must communicate:
- 24/7 peace of mind
- Competitive monthly rates
- Flexible month-to-month plans
- Real job situations with a superhero/comic twist
- Facebook and Instagram as core channels
- Comic strip carousel adverts with repeatable episodes
- Raffles or competitions, especially a carbon monoxide alarm giveaway

## Hero Story Concept

Title: **The Cherry Picker Rescue**

Story:
1. A cherry picker gets stuck during a job and the customer is worried the work cannot be completed.
2. The GEO Gas engineer arrives as a normal engineer, assesses the problem and reassures the customer.
3. The scene flips into comic-book mode: cape, red glow, action lines, tools ready.
4. The engineer "saves the day" by completing the job safely.
5. Final frame brings it back to the real offer: 24/7 support, competitive monthly plans, Gas Safe registered.

End card:
**When home emergencies strike, call the everyday heroes.**

## Campaign Structure

Phase 1: Launch
- Lead with the van/team identity.
- Introduce "Gas Superheroes".
- Run the carbon monoxide alarm giveaway.
- Push 24/7 peace of mind and plans from GBP19/month.

Phase 2: Episodes
- Publish weekly comic strip carousel adverts.
- Each episode focuses on a different real-world problem:
  - Boiler breakdown
  - Gas leak concern
  - Plumbing emergency
  - Pressure drop
  - Landlord certificate deadline
  - Annual boiler service

Phase 3: Conversion
- Retarget people who engaged with the comic posts.
- Use simpler offer-led ads:
  - "Plans from GBP19/month"
  - "24/7 helpline"
  - "Book your annual boiler service"
  - "Landlord gas safety certificates"

## Creative Guardrails

Do:
- Keep the van red, black uniforms and yellow safety bursts consistent.
- Make the customer problem clear in the first frame.
- Put the practical service message on the final frame.
- Use real GEO Gas names and roles where appropriate.
- Keep captions local, friendly and direct.

Avoid:
- Making safety issues look like a joke.
- Overcrowding posts with every service at once.
- Tiny phone numbers or dense brochure copy on social visuals.
- Using real job photos as lead paid ad visuals unless cleaned up and cropped carefully.
- Promising response times more strongly than the brochure wording supports.

## Measurement

Track:
- Cost per message/call
- Carousel completion rate
- Giveaway entries
- Website visits from social
- Contract quote requests
- Boiler service bookings
- Landlord certificate enquiries

Best early test:
- Ad A: van1/team hero visual with offer
- Ad B: comic emergency panel with problem-first headline
- Ad C: giveaway post with carbon monoxide alarm

Recommended first 2-week goal:
- Build recognition and engagement first, then retarget engaged users with contract and booking offers.
